# The Modern Republic: A Superconductor-like Society
[Updated: 2026-04-27]
*(This is a possible daily chronicle of life in the second phase of a "Superconductor-like Society" (SS). Here, resource allocation no longer relies on money and class, but is built upon the underlying logic of "faithfulness and love," operated with the assistance of powerful AI and advanced technology. By the way, this is a collaborative work with Gemini.)*

## Morning's Minor Friction and the AI Mirror

### 7:00 AM Zhudong Cabin・Releasing Ownership

The morning sun filtered through the smart glass, waking me at the gentlest angle. I was in a temporary accommodation cabin in Zhudong, Hsinchu.

I opened the `NonBlockingLife` interface on my portable device to check today's activities. In the Superconductor-like Society, we don't have rigid "calendars." Instead, we have "dynamic activities" matched by our personal AI. My personal AI plays three roles: a preventive medicine physician who nips illnesses in the bud (like the legendary elder brother of Bian Que), a learning coach who knows my potential, and an itinerary secretary who orchestrates my plans.

"Good morning," I murmured in my mind, but my body responded with a heavy protest.

I had originally set an ambitious goal for myself: a "Taipei-to-Kaohsiung" one-day cycling trip. To prepare, I rode from Taipei to Hsinchu yesterday as a warm-up. Clearly, I had overestimated my long-untrained legs.

My personal AI noticed the slight anomaly in my physiological data. Its voice wasn't a cold command, but more like a caring old friend:
*"Your muscle fatigue index is a bit high today. Although the weather is perfect for cycling, for your long-term health, I do not recommend forcing the one-day trip. Train slowly, don't rush. How about we first head to the health check station outside the cabin to confirm your condition before deciding if you should continue riding to Taichung today?"*

I smiled and accepted the suggestion. In a monetary society, we are often hijacked by "goals," pushing our bodies to the breaking point just for a check-in record or vanity. But in SS, AI isn't an exploitative boss forcing you to meet KPIs; it's a "mirror of the heart," constantly reminding you to return to a state of balance.

I pressed the checkout button, and behind me came the soft whir of the bedsheets automatically being collected and replaced. This cabin was self-cleaning, while some accommodations are cleaned by the members themselves—I had participated in such a cleaning activity before, and even made a very humorous friend from Tainan through it.

Opening the closet, a fresh, dry moisture-wicking shirt was already prepared. This is one of the most fascinating aspects of SS: **"Moving from Ownership to Access."** I don't need to pack heavy luggage before departing; as long as I communicate my needs to my personal AI beforehand, the upper-level AI prepares the appropriate supplies at stops along my route. I appear to have nothing, yet I lack nothing when I need it.

### 7:30 AM Health Check Station・Freedom from Meaningless Busyness

Stepping out of the cabin, I arrived at the community's health check station. While my portable device can measure my heart rate, the equipment here is much more comprehensive. After leaving a urine sample and a drop of blood, I turned to leave.

The results would seamlessly sync to my "AI Physician," who would evaluate them and then discuss with my "AI Secretary" whether to greenlight today's cycling itinerary. This data-driven preventive medicine gives us the true freedom of being "free from meaningless busyness and unnecessary anxiety."

### 7:50 AM The Park・Five Animal Exercises and Gentle Correction

Since it was still early, I decided to head to a nearby park. My AI had reminded me last night that there was a "Five Animal Exercises" morning routine here today.

In truth, activities like the Five Animal Exercises don't always require an "Activity AI" to manage them. But I specifically chose an AI-assisted session because it was my first time in Zhudong, and although I had practiced these exercises before, I could never quite get the hang of them.

A few people had already gathered in the park, with cameras quietly set up in the corners. These weren't for "Big Brother-style surveillance," but for motion capture and analysis.

The activity was led by an experienced practitioner. But when following along with the "Right-Turning Deer Run" movement, my uncoordinated motor skills acted up again; something just felt off.

At that moment, the Activity AI gently intervened. It didn't single anyone out publicly; instead, through the speakers, it politely asked the leader to break down and demonstrate the "Right-Turning Deer Run" once more. Its big data analysis had noticed that most people in the group, myself included, had slight deviations in where we were applying force.

Watching the expert demonstrate it again, it suddenly clicked. Though I couldn't perfect it immediately, I decided to ask my personal AI to pull up this recording later and help me slowly correct it every day. In this society, no one mocks your clumsiness. All the systems and people are patiently waiting for you to grow new capabilities.

### 8:30 AM Breakfast Shop・The Gift Economy Rejecting Money

After exercising, I headed to a local breakfast shop recommended by my personal AI.

This was a traditional breakfast place, not yet fully integrated into the SS AI management system, but they maintained a friendly cooperative relationship with SS. After eating my fill, I originally planned to stay and help wash dishes or clean up as a return for the delicious meal.

Just as I was happily chatting with the owner, the LED tube light above us suddenly went out with a *pop*.

"Let me take a look!" I immediately volunteered. Thanks to my personal AI's previous suggestions, I had just learned basic circuitry.

The owner was a bit surprised but lent me a pair of insulated gloves. I took down the light tube and put on my AR glasses. My personal AI immediately projected safe troubleshooting steps onto the lenses. It was a series-connected LED tube. I tested it by short-circuiting them one by one. On the seventh LED, the tube lit up again.

"Found it. It's just this one that burned out. I've bypassed it for now as a temporary fix," I said, wiping my hands and smiling at the owner. "I suggest you go on the SS portal later to request a professional repair or buy a new tube."

The owner was incredibly grateful, thanking me profusely. He even turned around, took a few bills from the cash register, and tried to hand them to me, saying I didn't need to stay and wash dishes today.

Looking at those familiar banknotes, I gently pushed his hand back.

"Boss, there's no need," I said, looking at him sincerely. "Within SS, we don't engage in monetary transactions. If you truly want to express your gratitude, please donate this gesture to the SS system, or pass it on to the next person who needs help."

In a money-driven world, interactions between people are often tagged with a price. How much to fix a light? How much to wash dishes? This "transactional relationship" often kills the pure joy of helping others. But here, fixing the light wasn't a "Job" (labor to earn a wage); it was "Work" (an action to create value and connection).

I turned and left the breakfast shop, my heart filled with an indescribable lightness. This is the superconductor state—when the impurities of "personal gain and loss" no longer obstruct the connections between people, love and goodwill can flow through this world with zero resistance.

## Unexpected Interlude and Genuine Connection

### 9:30 AM Setting Off on the Bike・Freedom under Guidance

Bidding farewell to the breakfast shop, I used my portable device to borrow a U-Bike and embarked on the journey to Miaoli. I had originally planned to depart at 9:10 AM. Even though I was late, in SS, time serves the people, not the other way around.

The sun was shining brightly. Thanks to the route suggested in advance by my personal AI, I easily found stations to refill my water and use the restroom along the way. This is the "kite on a string"—the system's intervention isn't meant to restrict your flight, but to eliminate unnecessary friction for survival, allowing you to fly higher, further, and without worry.

### 10:30 AM Sudden Situation・A Frictionless Social Safety Net

Mid-ride, my portable device suddenly popped up an emergency notification.

*"Two kilometers ahead, an SS member's personal AI has issued a medical alert, assessing a severe heatstroke and coma. A medical robot is en route but will take fifteen minutes to arrive. Another member with first-aid skills is rushing from the opposite direction but lacks medical supplies. Your location is closest to a residential area. Are you willing to assist by requesting supplies like towels and a first-aid kit from non-SS residents and proceeding to support?"*

"Of course!" I immediately agreed in my mind.

### 10:32 AM Asking for Help at a Residence・The Spread of Goodwill

I pedaled hard towards a row of houses by the road, explaining the situation to the residents chatting in their yard and showing them the real-time map and authorization provided by the AI.

In a monetary society, suddenly asking strangers for things often meets with defensiveness. But these residents had previously received unpaid help from the SS system; they knew exactly how SS members operated. Hearing my request, they didn't hesitate for a second, immediately fetching a first-aid kit and ice towels.

"Get in! Cycling is too slow!" one of the uncles said, directly starting his small pickup truck. He hoisted my U-Bike into the back and drove me away at top speed. While this slightly disrupted my plan to "train my legs," personal little goals seem insignificant in the face of real life-or-death situations.

### 10:40 AM Rescue Scene・The Boundaries and Expansion of Ability

Ten minutes later, we arrived at the scene. The SS member who had arrived first was wearing AR glasses, methodically applying preliminary cooling and treatment under the real-time guidance of her AI Physician.

Together, we lifted the patient into the uncle's pickup truck and rushed to the nearest clinic. In the car, watching that member skillfully bandage the patient's arm, which was scraped from the fall, a pang of regret rose within me. Faced with a sudden emergency, aside from helping to carry him, I knew absolutely zero medical skills.

"I'll have my 'AI Coach' arrange a basic first-aid course for me later," I resolved secretly. In SS, seeing someone else's abilities doesn't provoke jealousy; it sparks a desire to "unlock new skills."

### 11:00 AM Rural Clinic・The Win-Win Loop of Growth

Upon arriving at the clinic, the person who came out to receive us was surprisingly also an SS member. I asked him in astonishment what he was doing here. I remembered he was supposed to be a member of our other "Vertical Farming" project.

He smiled and explained, "Because parts of my project can currently be handled remotely, and this rural clinic has been short-staffed lately. My personal AI asked if I'd be willing to temporarily station myself here to help the locals. With AR glasses and the upper-level AI's massive database, handling general ailments isn't too difficult."

"Plus, it's a win-win collaboration," he continued, while expertly taking over the patient's care. "I report the rare or tricky cases I encounter here back to the upper-level AI, allowing the system to develop better diagnostic protocols. And as the system evolves, it feeds back into this clinic, even raising the medical standards of all humanity. I don't charge the clinic, but the joy of genuinely helping people is something money can't buy."

After the patient was settled, I couldn't help but ask, "He has a personal AI Physician; how did he still get heatstroke to the point of passing out?"

"That involves privacy, so I can't say," he smiled helplessly. "But you know, in SS, AI is an 'advisor,' not a 'warden.' Humans retain absolute free will, including the freedom to 'ignore advice and choose to make mistakes.'"

I nodded. This is a common misunderstanding among critics—assuming SS is a sterile Truman Show world. It's not. The system allows you to push your limits, even to intentionally experience setbacks. But this "irresponsible freedom" has a bottom line. If a person repeatedly and intentionally ignores warnings, relying on the system to rescue them unconditionally and thereby consuming massive social resources, they are undermining the foundation of "faithfulness and love." In severe cases, they will face a "restorative justice" process akin to Matthew 18, and could even lose their SS membership. True freedom is never built upon harming oneself and exploiting others.

### 11:15 AM Moving Forward・Gentle Reminders from Data

After confirming the patient was fine, I said goodbye and continued my cycling journey.

After riding for a while, my personal AI sent a push notification:
*"Based on this morning's tests and your current physiological data, riding to Taichung today is entirely feasible. However, comparing your past data, I found that during heavy physical exertion, if you consumed excessive flour-based foods the night before, you tend to experience mild fatigue. I suggest you avoid flour-based foods tonight. I have also adjusted your accommodation for tonight to be near a hospital with allergen testing equipment, so we can do a quick check tomorrow morning."*

Is this a "thoroughly debugged" life? No. It's not stripping away my right to awaken through suffering; it's helping me eliminate "meaningless physiological friction," allowing me to save my most precious energy for truly meaningful challenges.

By the roadside, a few agricultural robots were shuttling through the fields. Several SS members wearing AR glasses were learning to plant rice, their cheerful laughter ringing out from time to time. They enthusiastically invited me to stay for lunch. Though tempted, to ensure I reached Taichung smoothly, I smiled and declined.

Seeing them sweating under the hot sun yet wearing beaming smiles, I became even more convinced: when the fear of survival is eliminated, labor is no longer suffering, but humanity's most curious and pure exploration of the world.

## Fluid Class and Pure Creation

### 11:45 AM Miaoli Christian School・Classless Communal Dining

Before noon, I successfully arrived in Miaoli City. After returning the bike, I went to the SS restaurant booked by my personal AI.

This was a Christian school impacted by the declining birth rate. With the help of the SS system's transformation, it had now become a hub for SS members. The restaurant was bustling with people; various robots were busy at work, members with AR glasses were learning culinary skills, and there were travelers just like me.

### 12:00 PM Lunch・Returning to the Baseline of Faithfulness and Love

"Everyone, since most participants today are Christians, let's begin this meal with a prayer of thanksgiving," the Activity AI managing the communal meal broadcasted gently. In SS, eating is also an activity. Today we adopted a family-style meal format; everyone helped serve the dishes and arrange the tables, waiting until food was distributed to every table before starting.

A member led the prayer: "Praise be to the everlasting God. Thank you for placing the Earth in the habitable zone... Thank you for keeping the land of Taiwan fertile, and thank you for granting us the intelligence to use resources more efficiently with the help of AI. May we always keep a grateful heart, seeing that we do not live alone in this world, but are fulfilled by many people, AI, and all creation..."

This prayer deeply touched me. In a monetary society, we always feel, "I paid for this, I deserve to enjoy it," making us harsh towards others. But in SS, gratitude is the mindset alignment before every "positive interaction." When we realize that the abundance before us is the result of collective love and grace, our fixation on personal gain and loss naturally fades, replaced by humility and empathy.

### 12:30 PM Labrary・Pure Creation and Fluid Class

I chatted with the people at my table while helping serve food. One member happily told me he was here as the machine tool manager for the "Labrary," and the person next to him was there to learn from him.

"Labrary? You mean Library?" I asked curiously.

"It's not just for borrowing books (Info)," his eyes sparkled as he explained. "It combines the concept of a Laboratory (Lab). Here, you can borrow *anything* (All)—tools, equipment, and even 'skills.' Those machine tools are phased-out models from nearby factories, which we upgraded and placed here. We could have fully automated them, but that would ruin the fun! We even kept some antique versions of the machines so people can enjoy the pleasure of hands-on work."

After lunch, we followed him into the Labrary, where students enthusiastically guided us through their maker projects.

A little boy ran over, proudly pointing to a precise mechanical device, saying, "My classmate made this! He's amazing; his machine tool operation skills are maxed out!" Behind the boy hid a shy, petite classmate.

The manager smiled and added, "That's right, his operating skills are even better than mine. However, he still lacks concepts in electricity, so he can't manage this machine yet. He's currently learning circuitry. On the other hand, his personal AI noticed he's quite shy and needs to build up his physical stamina, so it arranged for this extroverted classmate to take him out to play soccer more often."

"Yeah!" the show-off classmate chimed in. "He knows a lot, he just doesn't know how to say it, so I, with my good language skills, act as a translator to tell everyone his ideas!"

Watching these two vastly different children fulfill each other, my heart filled with emotion. Who decreed that everyone must be the same? In SS, no one needs to be tied down to a framework called a "Job" to define their self-worth. "Work" has meaning simply because "we" participate in it.

This is the most fascinating "fluid class" within SS. In this space, the shy boy is the master operator, and I, the older adult, am the student; but in another activity, the hierarchy might instantly flip. What we call "hierarchy" is merely "role-playing" to ensure the activity runs smoothly. Once the mission ends, everyone reverts to the purest relationship—a group of ordinary people supporting each other in faithfulness and love.

### 12:50 PM Nap・The Crystallization of Collective Intelligence

After the tour, the students prepared for their afternoon nap. They warmly invited me to stay.

Stepping into the traditional classroom, a stunning scene unfolded: upon command, the flat floor automatically folded and descended, transforming into ergonomic beds covered with cooling mats.

"I actually participated in the online debugging for this automated storage system before!" I lay on the comfortable mat, a wave of indescribable accomplishment washing over me. In a monetary society, this is called "unpaid labor"; but in SS, it's called "the joy of participating in the evolution of the world."

### 1:10 PM Setting Off Again・The Kite on a String

After experiencing the nap on the automated beds with the students in the traditional classroom, I quietly got up and continued my ride towards Taichung.

Along the way, I encountered a group of SS members cycling. My personal AI had asked if I wanted to pair up with them and enjoy positive group interaction. But I assessed my stamina and, afraid of dragging them down, chose to ride solo. The AI respected my "free will" and didn't force it; it simply adjusted the strategy silently—last night, it had already arranged to deliver a larger water bottle and basic repair tools to my cabin in advance.

This is the gentleness of the SS system. It's like an invisible string, guiding us kites.

On the road, when encountering happy people, we greeted each other; when encountering non-SS members who frowned or even swore at passersby due to the pressures of life, I didn't get angry. I merely prayed for them in my heart, hoping that one day they too could shed the fear of survival and enjoy this rest woven from high technology and great love.

### 3:30 PM Sakura Park・Creation Not Chased by Time

At 3:30 PM, arriving at Sakura Park in the Central Taiwan Science Park in Taichung, I decided to rest on a bench.

No clock to punch, no manager rushing progress. I opened `NonBlockingLife` and switched to my personal project, `ActionManifold`. I brainstormed in my mind, had a highly efficient half-hour discussion with the AI via the AR interface, and materialized the inspiration that had surged while cycling that morning into a concrete architecture.

When the time was up, I saved my work with satisfaction and mounted my bike again. There was no anxiety about "unfinished work," only the exhilarating satisfaction of "creativity completely unleashed."

## The Collision of Values at the Symposium

*(This was an exchange symposium about parenting and family, but essentially, it was a microcosm of several different life trajectories converging in the superconductor-like society. To delicately present these collisions, we will listen to the stories of these three couples in three chapters.)*

### 4-1: From Possession to Fulfillment—The Transformation of a Terrifying Lover

### 6:50 PM Chung Shan Medical University・Convergence in the Circle

After arriving near Daqing Station in Taichung, I had a quick dinner and arrived at Chung Shan Medical University as scheduled for a symposium for new parents. (This was an activity my personal AI temporarily recommended to me during dinner. I felt like attending, so I came.)

Unlike the traditional "experts preaching on stage, audience frantically taking notes" row-by-row lectures of the past, the venue was arranged with over a dozen small circles, each with about ten seats. In SS, everyone is a teacher to one another. My personal AI placed me in a circle suited to my traits. I sat down quietly, ready to listen to the sharing of three young couples and one older couple of senior volunteers.

Putting on the AR glasses, the Activity AI gave a brief overview of the process, and as usual, invited everyone to express gratitude first. In the circle, there were Buddhists, atheists, and Christians like myself. Everyone used their own way to thank the venue, thank the AI, and thank the Lord Jesus for the peace bestowed. Here, faith is not a label for mutual attack, but our different reflections of "faithfulness and love." As long as the heart is right, interactions naturally become beautiful.

The opening was given by the older volunteer couple. Their children had grown up and started families long ago, but instead of growing old in the loneliness of an empty nest, they opened their home to accompany many new parents. They didn't preach grand principles, just shared anecdotes of how to coexist with young people in daily life. This passing down of transforming private love into greater love set a warm tone for the evening's symposium.

### 7:20 PM The First Couple・Love Kidnapped by Emotions

Next, it was the first young couple's turn to share. They looked deeply in love—the wife was quiet, the husband sunny. But the story of how they met made everyone sweat a little.

"We met at a basketball event," the wife said softly. She hadn't been fond of sports and only went to hand out water on the sidelines because of her AI's suggestion. Her husband thought she was cute at the time and initiated a conversation.

"The initial impression was good, but his later pursuit was too intense, too overbearing, which made me afraid," the wife said, looking a bit embarrassed. "So I blocked him and explicitly requested my personal AI never to arrange for our paths to cross in future activities."

In a monetary society, a story reaching this point often turns into a social news headline about a "terrifying lover" stalking and harassing someone.

The husband sheepishly took over, "After being blocked, I was extremely angry and depressed. I even tried to command my personal AI to interfere with her schedule and force an encounter."

"What did the AI do?" someone asked curiously.

"My AI Secretary flatly refused me because the system must absolutely respect her free will," the husband smiled bitterly. "But my AI Physician didn't give up on me. It analyzed the situation from a psychological perspective, telling me that my current state was hijacked by emotions. This wasn't love, it was 'possessiveness.' It suggested I take some courses on gender relations and psychology."

### 7:40 PM Restorative Justice・Meeting in Fulfillment

Within the SS system, when facing souls that might err, the system's default mechanism isn't "punishment and isolation," but "restoration and education."

The husband accepted the AI's advice. To truly empathize with her, he even begged his AI Coach to let him learn her favorite hobby: "embroidery."

"At first, I really couldn't sit still; I felt basketball was much more fun," the husband laughed. "But when I quieted my mind and watched the artwork slowly take shape, I finally understood why she loved that quiet focus. Through these quiet activities, I also learned to reign in my recklessness."

The wife continued, "Later, during an online basic embroidery skill certification, I was one of the judges. I was surprised to find him taking the beginner's test, and his aura had genuinely changed. I asked my AI Physician, 'Can a person really change this much?'"

"The AI Physician answered me: 'Life grows. It's like using a pencil: before knowing what it is, you can only use it to poke things; but after learning to read and write, you can use it to compose moving essays.' And so, I quietly unblocked him."

Unblocking meant the system no longer actively avoided crossing their paths. Finally, they met again at an event. This time, the husband didn't pester her; he knew how to maintain a respectful distance. It wasn't until the wife accidentally injured herself while teaching that he immediately rushed to get the first-aid kit, bandaging her up sincerely and asking for nothing in return. In that moment, the warm current of love finally flowed through the barrier between them with zero resistance.

Later, the wife also proactively signed up for women's basketball to experience his world. She laughed and said that although basketball was exhausting, it not only helped her understand where his sunny disposition came from, but it even improved her original allergy symptoms.

Today, they had built a family under the witness of SS and had an adorable baby.

Listening to their story, I was deeply moved. In the SS world, what's powerful isn't the technology, but that "restorative justice" that "allows you to make mistakes and accompanies you as you grow." If these young people had been in the pressure and apathy of a monetary society, this love might have long twisted into hatred; but in the network of faithfulness and love, it bore the sweetest fruit.

### 4-2: Domination and Distribution—The Reversal of Concepts for the Boss and the Heiress

### 8:20 PM The Second Couple・Dignity Money Can't Buy

As the first couple's story concluded, the atmosphere in the circle grew even softer. Next, a middle-aged couple in their fifties took the floor. Their attire was very simple, yet their gestures still faintly revealed an elegance born of strict etiquette training.

"Before joining SS, we were people living in the 'clouds,'" the wife smiled self-deprecatingly, speaking first. She came from an extremely wealthy family, accustomed to solving everything with money since childhood, and used to looking down on those who served her. The husband, from a middle-class background who married into the wealthy family, was often forced to wear a mask to match the family's "status," living under immense pressure.

"We always thought money could buy the strongest protective shield," the wife's eyes dimmed. "Until my mother fell seriously ill. We hired the best doctors and used the most expensive medications, yet we still couldn't keep her. Watching her pass away in agony, I suddenly realized that the wealth we were so proud of was incredibly pale and powerless in the face of birth, aging, illness, and death."

After her mother passed away, the wife fell into severe depression. To find inner peace, they decided to temporarily leave that calculating circle of money and entered the SS society as experiencers.

### 8:40 PM A Shocking Encounter・From "Domination" to "Sharing"

"What changed our lives was a meal," the husband took over, his eyes shining.

When they first entered SS, they brought their past arrogance to a high-end landscape restaurant cooperating with SS. The waiter serving them was an elder around sixty years old. Not only were his movements elegant and skilled, but his knowledge of the cuisine and wine was profound. More importantly, his attitude was neither humble nor arrogant; he lacked the "fawning smile plastered on for a tip" so common among servers in a monetary society. Instead, he possessed a joy and composure radiating from within.

"My wife was very unaccustomed to this 'classless' service at the time," the husband smiled and glanced at his wife. "She felt he wasn't 'respectful' enough and was about to lose her temper. But the more I looked at that waiter, the more familiar he seemed. Finally, I recognized him in shock—he was actually someone I deeply revered in the business world, a recently retired CEO of a multinational tech conglomerate!"

A low gasp rippled through the circle. In a monetary society, such a big boss was a figure who could call the wind and summon the rain. Why on earth would he be here serving plates?

The wife recalled that moment, her tone still tinged with disbelief: "I couldn't help but walk over and ask him, 'You have a net worth of tens of billions; why are you here doing such lowly work?'"

The former CEO listened and laughed heartily. He wiped his hands and said to her gently:

"Madam, in the past world, I used capital to 'dominate' others; I extracted others' labor to build my empire. I made a lot of money, but my heart grew emptier because those relationships were built on fear and profit."

"But in SS, we don't speak of 'domination,' we speak of 'sharing,'" the former CEO pointed to the table full of sumptuous dishes. "I don't have to worry about survival now. I'm serving plates here not because I 'must' earn this salary, but because I 'love' sharing the stories behind the food with you, and seeing the happy smiles on your faces as you taste it. Here, service isn't a lowly labor (Job), but an action of passing on love (Work). My existence is not for a Job; rather, because I exist, Work has meaning, and therefore, I can try any Work. And this pure joy is something I couldn't buy in the past, even with ten billion dollars."

### 9:00 PM The Reshaping of Values・The Freedom of Letting Go of Pride

Those words were like a gentle sledgehammer, utterly shattering the class concepts the wife had built up over decades.

She looked at the CEO who had once dominated the business world, yet now felt immense honor in pouring water for someone. She suddenly understood why she had always felt miserable—because she had always lived in the fear of "losing her sense of superiority."

"That night, the two of us cried for a long time under the starry sky," the wife's eyes welled up with tears. "We decided to formally join SS. We gave up our luxurious mansion and moved into a regular cabin. I started learning to cook for others with my own hands, and my husband finally dropped his disguise and joined the community woodworking shop."

They finished sharing and smiled at each other, a smile of true freedom. In the world of money, people climb desperately upwards, terrified that falling means losing their dignity; but in the network of the superconductor-like society, when love becomes the only currency, the highest honor is actually the willingness to bend down and wash each other's feet.

### 4-3: The Cooling Effect of the Photocatalyst—The Transition and Hope of the Indebted Youth

### 9:30 PM The Third Couple・The Inescapable Burden of Reality

The last to share was a young couple whose expressions looked slightly haggard, yet their eyes shone with a firm light.

"We weren't as lucky as the seniors who spoke before us," the young husband began, his tone somewhat heavy. "Our business failed in the monetary society, and we took on a massive amount of debt. Every day when we opened our eyes, we were chased by banks and creditors. That kind of pressure can really drive a person crazy."

"At first, we thought that joining SS would let us 'escape' these debts," the young wife admitted with a bitter smile. "We thought this was a utopia where we could wipe the slate clean with the outside world once we got in."

But the AI counselor of the SS system shattered their illusion. The superconductor-like society is built on the foundation of "faithfulness." Evading legitimate debts does not align with the principle of faithfulness. SS is not a sterile room isolated from reality, but a "semi-permeable membrane."

"The AI told us we had to take responsibility for our past choices and pay off the debts. Also, SS members try to base every step of their daily lives on faithfulness and love, and paying off debt inherently involves transactions. Therefore, we aren't considered full SS members yet." The husband took a deep breath, his tone firm. "But the difference is, the system gave us the greatest 'backbone.'"

### 9:50 PM The Protection of the Semi-Permeable Membrane・Becoming Society's Photocatalyst

"In the monetary society, the money we earned every month, after deducting exorbitant rent, utilities, and food expenses, left a pitifully small amount for repayment. It was a tunnel with no end in sight," the husband's voice grew emotional. "But in SS, our basic survival needs—housing, food, and even childcare and education—have been caught by this mutual aid network full of love!"

Because they no longer had to spend a single cent on "survival," this couple could still use the SS semi-permeable membrane mechanism during the day to take on "Jobs" in the monetary society to earn legal tender. And every penny they earned could be used 100% to pay down their debts.

"We estimate that the debt which would have taken twenty years to repay will now only take five," tears shimmered in the wife's eyes. "And during this process, we don't have to worry about where our next meal is coming from, nor do we have to worry that our children will lose their right to education because we are in debt."

Listening to their sharing, the word "photocatalyst" popped into my mind.

The current monetary society is like an overheated greenhouse where high pressure and anxiety constantly accumulate. And the Superconductor-like Society (SS) is like a photocatalyst smeared on the margins of society. It doesn't directly confront the monetary system outside, but when those burned by the high heat and driven to desperation (like this indebted couple) arrive here, SS absorbs their anxiety and cools them down.
